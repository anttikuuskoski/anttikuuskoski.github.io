Kotia Finland Inc., Founder & CEO
2011 ->

Founded the company to continue working with our former key clients. Our plans turned sour quickly, as we had relied on a single customer who decided to cut burn big time. Change was imminent and heavy restructuring followed.

2012 - TEKES provided us with an R&D loan for developing Kotia.com - then the "social media of home". Well, develop we did - failing to validate our propositions in advance. Nope, no lean startup back then, silly us...

2013-2015 - Heavy pivoting several times over eventually focusing on a marketplace for provably good real estate agents. Eventually realized there was no market. While trying, created methods and products for collection of real estate agent CX and ways to improve it. Grew quite adept at digital marketing but stopped when realized our value prop was weak.

2016 - Two brothers left in the company; time to acknowledge failure, gather the learnings and move on.

Key products
  • Kotia.com - marketplace for good real estate agents. Focus on areas such as personal branding, SE / Social marketing, utilization & publishing of testimonials and customer satisfaction indicators.
  • MONITOR - real estate agent CX survey, analysis, utilization. Built a nice engine for collecting and analyzing agent CX data and offered consultation on how to turn data into money.
Key activities / experiences
  • Wasted 000's on buying digital marketing services.
  • Blogged, shared, advertised, measured and optimized; spent time and further money but learned to do SEM / SEO / social.
  • Collected CX from 1000+ real estate agents' clients and understood what's wrong with the market (and our business) and what could be done about it.
  • Utilized CX data successfully in social / search channels to pull in traffic
  • Designed an email feedback collection flow with 30%+ response rate.
  • Designed a telephone feedback collection flow with 80%+ response rate.
Key businesswise learnings
  • If you're unable to create a true value prop, quit early.
  • Your value prop cannot depend on anyone else's
  • Ensure non-conflicting values on both sides of the business model
  • Understand the structural nature of client companies
  • Ensure there's a big enough market

2010 - Decided to restructure; stepped down from board and continued as a COO; hired a full time assistant, project manager etc. All hell breaks loose; new board tosses away company values and the newly appointed COO has no longer say in company operations. By the end of year, a non-graceful exit to a new company was decided and acted upon.

By the middle of 2011, the company was no longer active, as all revenue generating owners had left the building.
